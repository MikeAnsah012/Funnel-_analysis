# End-to-End Gaming Funnel & Monetization Analysis

# Dashboard

# Executive Summary
This project analyzes the player journey from install to purchase, identifying key drop-off points and revenue opportunities within the gaming funnel. Out of 1.2K players, early engagement is strong, with 90% progressing from install to game open. However, onboarding shows friction, as only 72% complete the tutorial. The largest gap occurs at monetization, with just 17% of players converting to paying users, highlighting a key opportunity to improve purchase strategies. PC is the leading platform, generating 43% of revenue, while EMEA is the top region with a 32% share.

# Business Problem
The business problem is to understand where and why players drop off within the user journey, and how these drop-offs impact overall conversion and revenue. Despite strong initial engagement, low conversion to paying users limits revenue growth, making it critical to identify friction points in onboarding and optimize monetization strategies.

# Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze player progression through the gaming funnel to measure conversion rates at each stage, identify key drop-off points, and evaluate monetization performance in order to improve user retention and increase revenue.

# Methodology
Data was cleaned and standardized using SQL, including handling inconsistent formats and currency normalization. Key KPIs such as ARPU and ARPPU were calculated, followed by funnel analysis to measure conversion rates and identify drop-off points. Revenue was segmented by region, platform, and item to uncover key drivers. Insights were then presented through an interactive Excel dashboard.

# Skills
- SQL: Data extraction, cleaning, and transformation (JOINs, CASE, CTEs, aggregations)
- Data Cleaning: Handling missing values, standardizing formats, currency normalization using REGEXP
- Data Analysis: Funnel analysis, conversion rate calculation, revenue segmentation
- KPI Development: ARPU, ARPPU, total revenue, conversion metrics
- Data Visualization: Building interactive dashboards in Excel (charts, slicers, KPI cards)
- Business Analysis: Identifying drop-offs, revenue drivers, and optimization opportunities
- Data Storytelling: Translating analysis into actionable insights and recommendations.

# Results
The analysis shows strong initial engagement, with 90% of users progressing from install to game open. However, onboarding presents a drop-off, as only 72% complete the tutorial. The largest gap occurs at monetization, with just 17% of players converting to paying users, highlighting a key opportunity to improve purchase conversion. PC is the top-performing platform, generating 43% of total revenue, while EMEA leads all regions with 32%. The gap between ARPU and ARPPU also indicates that revenue is driven by a relatively small group of high-spending users.

# Business Recommendations
- 72% tutorial completion indicates onboarding friction—simplify tutorials and add guidance or early rewards to improve retention.
- Only 17% convert to paying users—optimize monetization with targeted offers and better-timed purchase prompts.
- PC generates 43% of revenue—prioritize updates, marketing, and exclusive content for this platform.
- EMEA contributes 32% of revenue—expand localized marketing and region-specific promotions.
- Battle Pass and Skin Packs drive the most revenue—boost visibility through bundles and in-game promotion.
- Revenue is concentrated among high spenders—use player segmentation to tailor offers and maximize value.
- Implement automated dashboards to continuously track funnel performance and conversion trends.
